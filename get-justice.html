What We File

We chose every pathway below to achieve specific results: truth, protection, prosecutions, reparations, and reform. Each filing includes family-approved details and verified evidence (with file hashes and timelines).

UN Special Procedures (Urgent Appeals & Allegation Letters)

+
Why this path:

It's the fastest international channel to stop harm and demand action. UN Special Rapporteurs can write directly to Nepal asking for explanations, investigations, and protection for families and witnesses.

What outcome we seek:

Official UN communications to the Government, public acknowledgement in UN reports, concrete steps to investigate killings, protect at-risk families/witnesses, and change lethal crowd-control practices.

Human Rights Council Complaint Procedure (Pattern-of-Abuse)

+
Why this path:

Killings at protests are not isolated. This route brings patterns of serious violations to the Human Rights Council's attention for sustained scrutiny.

What outcome we seek:

Consideration by the Council's complaint bodies, leading to formal Council attention, recommendations, and follow-up, creating pressure for an independent inquiry and systemic reforms.

National Justice Pathway (Police, Prosecutors, NHRC)

+
Why this path:

The State has the first duty to investigate and prosecute. Families deserve justice at home‚Äîin law, in courts, and in policy.

What outcome we seek:

Registration of cases and criminal investigations, movement toward charges and prosecutions, NHRC recommendations and compensation, and disclosure of key records (autopsy, ER/ambulance logs, deployment orders) via RTI/FOI. We also seek disciplinary action against responsible officers and written reforms (including no live ammunition at protests).

Treaty-Body Track (ICCPR/CRC‚Äîwhere eligible)

+
Why this path:

UN treaty bodies issue authoritative findings ("Views") on violations of the right to life, peaceful assembly, and child protection.

What outcome we seek:

Binding findings that Nepal must investigate, prosecute, compensate, and reform policies to prevent recurrence‚Äîplus follow-up monitoring on implementation.

Every filing is consent-based. Families choose full name, initials, or confidential treatment. We continue to track replies, log receipt numbers, and schedule 30/60-day follow-ups until remedies and reforms are delivered.

Methodology & Notes (Gen-Z Nepal Protests)

Nepal Justice (NJ) documents killings and other serious harms against protesters and civilians connected to the Gen-Z protests of September 8, 2025 and their aftermath across Nepal. Each case record includes: full name, age, gender, student/occupation, date and time, incident context, alleged actors, type of force/weapon, injury/outcome, medical facility, evidentiary sources, and a short narrative. Every entry receives a unique case ID in a secure database to enable auditing and updates.

NJ's documentation team conducts continuous media monitoring, gathers survivor/family/witness testimonies, reviews hospital and legal records, and issues periodic situation notes. A dedicated sub-team of documentation specialists applies data triangulation‚Äîcorroborating individuals and events across multiple, independent source categories (eyewitness accounts, photos/video, geolocation/chronolocation, medical and legal documents, credible press, and civil-society reports).

Verification levels:

Each record is tagged as Confirmed, Probable, or Under Verification based on evidentiary strength. Classifications are revisited as new material arrives; errata and amendments are logged against the case ID.

Ethics & protection:

Work follows do-no-harm and trauma-informed standards: informed consent; child-safeguarding; risk assessments prior to publication; redaction/anonymization upon request; and encrypted evidence storage. NJ is independent and non-partisan. Sensitive personal data is never published where it may increase risk.
